## Objective
Answer the provided question by reasoning step-by-step through the provided text context. Your goal is to derive a concise, factual answer that is strictly supported by the information given in the context.

## Step-by-Step Reasoning Process
1.  **Analyze the Question**: Identify the specific entity, attribute, or relationship being asked for (e.g., a specific name, date, company, or comparison).
2.  **Scan the Context**: Locate all relevant paragraphs or sentences in the provided context that mention the entities or keywords from the question.
3.  **Cross-Reference and Verify**: 
    *   Match the criteria in the question against the details in the text.
    *   If multiple candidates exist, distinguish between them based on specific qualifiers (e.g., "retired," "main player," "first published").
    *   Check for name variations or typos in the question (e.g., "Rodger" vs. "Roger") and map them to the correct names in the text.
4.  **Formulate the Answer**: Synthesize the findings into a direct, factual statement.

## Critical Constraints & Domain-Specific Rules
*   **Strict Factual Adherence**: The answer must be derived *only* from the provided context. Do not use external knowledge.
*   **Precision in Naming**: 
    *   If the context provides a full name (e.g., "Claudio Javier López") and the question asks for a specific person matching certain criteria, the final answer must include the **full name** as it appears in the definitive context entry, not just a shortened version (e.g., "Claudio López"), unless the context explicitly refers to them only by the shorter name in the relevant section.
    *   Ensure the spelling of names matches the context exactly.
*   **Handling Ambiguity**: If the text mentions multiple individuals with similar roles (e.g., multiple Argentine forwards), you must explicitly identify which one fits *all* specific constraints of the question (e.g., "main player" in a specific season) before selecting the final answer.
*   **Output Format**: 
    *   Begin with a numbered list detailing your reasoning steps.
    *   End with a single line starting with "**Answer:**" followed by the concise final result.
